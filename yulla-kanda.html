The Yulla Kanda Trek is a hidden gem situated in the majestic Kinnaur region of Himachal Pradesh. Renowned for its spectacular meadows and profound religious significance, this trail leads trekkers to the highest Krishna temple in the world, located precisely in the middle of a stunning holy lake.

Standing at an altitude of approximately 12,778 feet, Yulla Kanda is considered an excellent moderate-level Himalayan trek. It offers an incredible blend of spirituality, deep valleys, high mountain passes, and the raw beauty of Kinnaur, making it a unique expedition off the commercial tourist circuit.

Best Time To Visit Yulla Kanda

The ideal season for the Yulla Kanda Trek is from May to October. During the summer months, trekkers can witness blooming meadows and clear panoramic mountain views. The weather remains pleasant, and the skies are generally crystal-clear, offering stunning photography opportunities.

Difficulty Level

Yulla Kanda is classified as a moderate trek. While determined beginners can complete it successfully, basic fitness preparation and cardiovascular endurance are highly recommended. Trekkers should be comfortable walking uphill on uneven terrain for several hours to reach the sacred lake.

Altitude

The highest point of the trek is the Yulla Kanda Temple, seated at approximately 12,778 feet above sea level. Proper acclimatization and hydration at the base village of Yulla Khas are important before making the final ascent.

How To Reach

The base camp is Yulla Khas village. Most trekkers begin their journey via an overnight drive from Delhi or Chandigarh, traveling along the scenic Kinnaur-Tibet highway via Shimla.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Yulla Kanda suitable for beginners?

Yes, physically active beginners can complete this trek with proper guidance and pacing.

How many days are required?

Most itineraries require 3 Days and 2 Nights from the base camp.

What is the maximum altitude?

Approximately 12,778 feet at the holy lake and temple.

Are there network signals available?

Network connectivity is highly unreliable beyond Yulla Khas village.
