Day-Wise Itinerary

1

Day 1: Manali to Jobra — Trek to Chika Camp

Your adventure begins from Manali Mall Road at around 9:00 AM. After a scenic drive of approximately 22 km to Jobra, the trekking journey starts through beautiful pine forests, river crossings, and lush landscapes. The 4 km trek takes around 4-5 hours to Chika Camp (3,078m).

Meals: Lunch, Evening Snacks & Dinner | Stay: Alpine Camps
2

Day 2: Chika Camp to Balu Ka Ghera

Today's trail passes through expansive green meadows, glacier-fed streams, and breathtaking mountain landscapes. The trek covers approximately 8 km and takes around 4-5 hours to reach an altitude of 3,783 metres.

3

Day 3: Balu Ka Ghera to Shea Goru via Hampta Pass

Ascent towards the magnificent Hampta Pass (4,310m). Witness the stunning transition from the lush green Kullu Valley to the barren landscapes of Spiti Valley. Descend towards Shea Goru Camp.

4

Day 4: Shea Goru to Chhatru — Excursion to Chandratal

Trek downhill towards Chhatru. Continue by vehicle towards the mesmerising Chandratal Lake (4,300m). After exploring the lake, return to Chhatru for an overnight stay.

5

Day 5: Chhatru to Manali via Atal Tunnel

Begin the return journey to Manali through the world-famous Atal Tunnel. Reach Manali by approximately 3:00 PM.

The Hampta Pass Trek is one of the most beautiful crossover treks in the Indian Himalayas. Located in Himachal Pradesh, this trek connects the lush green Kullu Valley with the dramatic barren landscapes of Lahaul and Spiti. The trail offers an incredible variety of scenery including pine forests, waterfalls, alpine meadows, river crossings, glaciers and high mountain passes.

Standing at an altitude of approximately 14,100 feet, Hampta Pass is considered one of the best moderate-level Himalayan treks for beginners and experienced trekkers alike. The changing landscapes throughout the journey make it unique among Indian trekking routes.

Best Time To Visit Hampta Pass

The ideal season for the Hampta Pass Trek is from June to September. During these months trekkers can witness blooming meadows, clear mountain views and pleasant temperatures. July and August offer lush greenery while September provides crystal-clear skies and stunning mountain photography opportunities.

Difficulty Level

Hampta Pass is classified as a moderate trek. While beginners can complete it successfully, basic fitness preparation is highly recommended. Trekkers should be comfortable walking 5–7 hours daily on uneven terrain and climbing steep sections near the pass.

Altitude

The highest point of the trek is Hampta Pass at approximately 14,100 feet above sea level. Proper acclimatization and hydration are important throughout the journey.

How To Reach

Most trekkers begin their journey from Manali. The nearest airport is Bhuntar Airport near Kullu, while Chandigarh and Delhi provide the most convenient rail and road connections.

Packing List

  • Trekking shoes with good grip
  • Warm jacket
  • Raincoat or poncho
  • Trekking pole
  • Water bottle
  • Sunscreen and sunglasses
  • Personal medicines
  • Headlamp

Mobile Network Availability

Mobile connectivity is available in Manali but becomes unreliable during the trek. Most campsites have little or no network coverage.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Hampta Pass suitable for beginners?

Yes. It is considered one of the best Himalayan treks for first-time trekkers.

How many days are required?

Most Hampta Pass itineraries are completed in 4–5 days.

What is the maximum altitude?

Approximately 14,100 feet.

Can children join the trek?

Children above 12 years with good fitness can generally participate.
